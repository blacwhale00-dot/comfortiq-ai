The evidence

Before brand. Before efficiency.
Before even price.

The single most important factor in your next HVAC system is choosing a reputable, trustworthy contractor. That's not a slogan — it's what the research says.

Up to 30% of a system's efficiency is lost to poor installation alone. A federal study found improper installation can drive heating and cooling energy use up by roughly a third — the best equipment in the world can't outrun a bad install.

Source: U.S. National Institute of Standards & Technology (NIST)

More than half of new HVAC systems are not installed correctly — wrong sizing, bad refrigerant charge, leaky ducts. The EPA created its Quality Installation guidelines because this is the industry's biggest hidden problem.

Source: EPA ENERGY STAR — HVAC Quality Installation

The industry is changing under your feet. Private-equity firms have rolled up hundreds of local HVAC companies since 2022. Veteran technicians describe quota-driven "replace more boxes" cultures after acquisition — one engineer at a rolled-up firm estimated warranty calls doubled. The company name on the truck may not mean what it meant five years ago.

Source: The American Prospect (2023), "Private Equity Intensifies Rollups of HVAC Installers" Source: The Wall Street Journal (2024) on the PE rush into home services

That's why the checklist matters more than ever: NATE-certified technicians, and a real ACCA Manual J load calculation — the residential building code's own standard for sizing — instead of a guess off the old unit's sticker. Oversized systems short-cycle and leave your air sticky; undersized ones never catch up on a July afternoon. Your verified Guzzler Score includes that load calc, so you can check any bid against it.

Source: ACCA Manual J — Residential Load Calculation Source: 2021 International Residential Code (equipment sizing per Manual J) Source: NATE — North American Technician Excellence
